# Review Criteria

## Scientific Question

- Is the scientific question explicit, non-trivial, and practically motivated?
- Can the contribution be stated in one sentence?
- Does the problem require a new method, mechanism, theory, system, or experiment?
- Are the physical, operational, behavioral, algorithmic, or statistical mechanisms clear?
- Is the target outcome a scientific insight, a decision improvement, a prediction improvement, or an engineering system?

## Novelty Classification

Classify each claimed contribution as one of:

- true scientific innovation;
- new mechanism or problem formulation;
- new algorithmic idea;
- new theoretical result;
- new empirical finding;
- new coupling between subsystems;
- new constraint/objective/latent variable;
- engineering integration;
- application transfer;
- architecture replacement;
- parameter tuning;
- presentation-level novelty only.

## Mathematical / Algorithmic / Modeling Rigor

Check assumptions, variables, parameters, objectives, losses, constraints, algorithmic steps, units, boundary conditions, uncertainty modeling, complexity, feasibility, optimality, and convergence evidence where relevant.

## Experimental Credibility

Check claim-experiment mapping, baseline strength, ablation completeness, sensitivity analysis, multiple random seeds, statistical significance, robustness, extreme scenarios, computational efficiency, leakage, future information, and consistency between metrics and real-world value.

## Publication Readiness Scale

- Not submission-ready: core problem, method, or experiments are incomplete.
- Ordinary journal: clear engineering work but limited novelty or weak validation.
- SCI Q2-like: complete workflow, moderate novelty, acceptable validation.
- SCI Q1-like: clear novelty, strong baselines, rigorous experiments, convincing discussion.
- Top-journal candidate: important problem, differentiated mechanism, rigorous model, comprehensive validation, strong generality, and clear scientific contribution.
- Workshop/short-paper candidate: interesting preliminary idea, but incomplete validation or narrow scope.
- Solid conference candidate: clear technical contribution and convincing experiments, but limited breadth or depth.
- Top-conference candidate: sharp novelty, strong positioning, rigorous experiments, reproducible implementation, and clear technical significance.

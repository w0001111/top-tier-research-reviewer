# Top-Tier Review Patterns

## Common Weaknesses

- The contribution is a model combination rather than a new idea.
- The problem is important, but the method does not exploit the problem structure.
- The method improves metrics but does not validate the claimed mechanism.
- Baselines are outdated or unfairly tuned.
- Ablations do not correspond to claimed contributions.
- The paper claims generality but tests only one narrow dataset.
- The paper claims robustness but lacks distribution shift or stress tests.
- The method is too complex for the size of the demonstrated gain.
- The writing overclaims beyond the evidence.

## Top-Journal Strength Signals

- The scientific question is important and clearly framed.
- The method formalizes a real mechanism or constraint.
- Experiments validate both performance and mechanism.
- Robustness and limitations are honestly treated.
- The contribution generalizes beyond one dataset.
- The paper provides insight, not only a tool.

## Top-Conference Strength Signals

- The novelty is sharp and easy to state.
- The technical idea is differentiated from recent work.
- The experiments are focused and convincing.
- The method is reproducible and clearly implemented.
- Ablations isolate the actual new component.
- The paper answers likely reviewer objections directly.
